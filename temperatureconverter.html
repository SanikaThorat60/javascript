Temperature Into Farenheit :

What Is Celsius??

Celsius is a scale (sometimes called Centigrade) that is used in many countries as a unit of measurement for temperature. The Celsius scale was created by a Swedish astronomer called Anders Celsius. On the scale, 0 degrees (°) Celsius is the melting point of pure water at sea level, at normal pressure.

What Is Fahrenheit??

Fahrenheit is a scale for measuring temperature, in which water freezes at 32 degrees and boils at 212 degrees. It is represented by the symbol ° F. By mid-morning, the temperature was already above 100 degrees Fahrenheit.
Celsius To Farenheit Conversion:

°F equals °C multiplied by 9/5, plus 32
°F = °C * 9/5 + 32

Formula to convert Celsius to Fahrenheit F = C x 9/5 + 32

How To Convert Celsius To Farenheit

  1. Multiply degrees Celsius by 1.8 (or 9/5)
  2. Add 32 to the product from step 1

For example,

to convert 21.5 degrees Celsius (centigrade) to degrees Fahrenheit, we plug our numbers into the formula as shown below:
°F = (°C × (9/5)) + 325
°F = (21.5 × (9/5)) + 32
°F = (21.5 × 1.8) + 32
°F = 38.7 + 32
°F = 70.7
21.5 degrees Celsius is equal to 70.7 degrees Fahrenheit.
